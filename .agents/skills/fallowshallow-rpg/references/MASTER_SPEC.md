# Fallows Hallow: RPG Master Specification (Unified)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Core Architecture & Mechanics)
**Engine Target:** Custom TypeScript + PixiJS (16-bit JRPG Aesthetic)
**AI Orchestrator:** Taliesin v3.0 (Gemma 4 via local FastAPI Bridge)
**Lead Architect Persona:** SAKAGUCHI

---

## 1. Narrative & Systemic Vision
*Fallows Hallow* is a traditional, narrative-heavy turn-based RPG where technical systems serve the emotional weight of a fixed canonical storyline (The "Rails"). While the plot is deterministic, the *flavor* of the journey is generated dynamically by **Taliesin**, creating a unique experience based on party composition and choices.

### 1.1 The Golden Thread
The story follows a group of children (The Chroni lineage) who are drawn into the "Old World"—a magical realm where they are harvested to power a cosmic prison for the god Mathias. The gameplay loop alternates between linear story "beats" and dynamic party interactions powered by the local LLM.

---

## 2. Technical Architecture (The Steel)
*   **Core Engine:** Custom TypeScript ECS (Entity-Component-System).
*   **Rendering:** PixiJS for high-performance 2D WebGL (16-bit SNES style).
*   **AI Bridge:** An asynchronous `TaliesinManager` fetches structured JSON dialogue from a local Python FastAPI server wrapping Gemma 4.
*   **UI System:** A classic JRPG portrait/text box system driven by JSON payloads (e.g., `{"speaker": "Gideon", "emotion": "angry", "text": "..."}`).

---

## 3. The "Lagi Tapestry" Magic System
The traditional MP system is replaced by the **Lagi Tapestry**, where magic is fueled by **Memory Forging**.

### 3.1 Soul Canvas & Pigments
*   **Mechanic:** Each character has a Soul Canvas composed of Red (Passion), Blue (Sorrow), Gold (Joy), and Green (Nature) pigments.
*   **Forging:** Casting high-tier "Lagi" spells requires permanently sacrificing or altering these pigments. This "Forging" process directly influences the dialogue generated by Taliesin, as the character's memories (and thus their personality) shift throughout the game.
*   **Full Circle Gauge:** A rhythmic gauge that builds during ATB combat. When filled, it allows the party to execute "Synchronized Traces," granting latent powers to those who lack natural magical affinity.

---

## 4. The Ensemble: Latent Powers & Capabilities
Each child possesses a unique capability that fundamentally alters combat strategy.

### 4.1 John (The Seeker / Temporal Savant)
*   **Role:** Frontline / Timeline Anchor.
*   **Power:** Temporal Sight. John can manipulate the ATB timeline, delaying enemy actions or hastening ally turns. He is the "Linear Anchor" who must survive to ensure the lineage continues.

### 4.2 Gideon (The Beaut / Martial Singularity)
*   **Role:** High-Evasion Physical DPS.
*   **Power:** Martial Singularity. Once his Full Circle gauge is maximized, he transforms into "The Beaut," ignoring all enemy defenses and physical resists. Outside of this state, he relies on "Desperate Strikes" and evasion.

### 4.3 Nicci (The Guardian / Nature Matriarch)
*   **Role:** Environment Controller.
*   **Power:** Natural Attunement. Nicci does not cast direct damage. She manipulates the battlefield (e.g., rooting enemies with vines, summoning Old World beasts) and provides nature-based buffs.

### 4.4 Alfred (The Oracle / Prophetic Scholar)
*   **Role:** Analyst / Pre-Cognitive Mage.
*   **Power:** Prophecy. Alfred sees enemy intents 2 turns ahead. His "Past" spells can revert HP/State to previous turns, while "Future" spells set conditional traps.

### 4.5 The Twins (Amanda & Brittany / Linked Casters)
*   **Role:** Dual-State Synergists.
*   **Power:** Telepathic Link. They act as a single entity on the grid. They share buffs/debuffs instantly.
*   **Takeover Mechanic:** One twin can sacrifice her turn to allow the other to inhabit her body, granting double stats and access to "Linked Spells" for a limited duration.

## 5. Systemic Mechanics (SAKAGUCHI Deep-Dive)

### 5.1 Progression: Activity-Based Growth (FFII Inspired)
Characters do not gain "Experience Points" in the traditional sense. Stats grow based on battle behavior:
*   **Strength:** Increases when performing physical attacks.
*   **Stamina:** Increases when taking damage or surviving near-death.
*   **Lagi (Magic):** Increases when casting spells. Higher tier "Lagi" forging has a chance to permanently decrease one pigment while drastically boosting the spell's potency.
*   **Speed:** Increases when acting frequently in the ATB loop.

### 5.2 Combat: The Row System & ATB
*   **The Grid:** A 2-row layout (Front/Back).
*   **Logic:**
    - **Front Row:** 100% Physical Damage dealt, 100% Physical Damage taken.
    - **Back Row:** 50% Physical Damage dealt (unless using ranged/magic), 50% Physical Damage taken.
*   **ATB Math:** `ActionGate = CurrentTime * (Speed / Constant)`. When `ActionGate >= 100`, the entity chooses an action.

### 5.3 Taliesin Feedback Loop
Every mechanical change (e.g., John's Strength increases to Tier 2) triggers a Taliesin event. The next time the party talks, John will sound more confident or aggressive in his narration.

## 6. Gameplay Loop: The Taliesin Pulse
1.  **Exploration:** Grid-based tile movement in the Old World.
2.  **Context Harvest:** The engine gathers the current state (Location: "The Woods", Inventory: "Pillowcase of Candy", Party: ["John", "Alfred"]).
3.  **The Bridge:** The engine sends this to the Taliesin Host via `POST /generate_dialogue`.
4.  **The Reveal:** Gemma 4 returns the party's reaction to the environment, maintaining their character voices and adhering to the "Story Bible" ledgers.
5.  **Combat (ATB):** Triggered by encounters. Transition to high-spectacle 2D combat using programmatic VFX.
