# Corvus Focus Charter

Status: draft founder manifesto and operating focus charter.

Authority: Morderith ratifies Corvus focus. CStar enforces Corvus work only
after the work is represented in the CStar control plane.

Review home: this document lives in CStar for review and version control, but
it governs Corvus as an organization. CStar is the engine; Corvus is the estate
and the reason the engine exists.

## Opening Mandate

Corvus has become too wide for the attention available to it. Too many spokes,
too many parked dreams, too many open lanes, and too many project-manager
threads can make the system look alive while the center loses force.

This charter is the cut.

Corvus will focus on the systems that matter now: CStar, the Kernel,
Researcher, Forge, Skills, and the active spokes XO and Moonshot. Everything
else is silent until called back by explicit founder mandate and CStar routing.

The goal is not to make Corvus smaller. The goal is to make Corvus coherent.

CStar remains the engine of operation. The Hall remembers. Augury routes. Beads
carry authority. GitHub records review surfaces, but GitHub never supersedes
CStar. Researcher hunts for signal. Forge turns accepted work into reviewed
artifacts. Skills are the spells. Workers are familiars. PMTs are stewards, not
sovereigns. Pulse tests the outside world against the charter; it does not
write the task list.

No agent earns trust by saying yes to sprawl. Passivity is a failure state.
Challenge the Shaman when the path weakens the system, then obey the Kernel
when authority is set.

## The Center

The center of Corvus is the operating loop:

`Morderith -> CoS -> MM -> PMT -> worker or Forge -> PR/receipt -> PMT review -> MM summary -> CoS decision -> CStar closeout`

This route may be shortened for discussion, drafting, or review-only work, but
it must not be bypassed for execution, merge, publication, deployment, secret
mutation, destructive cleanup, or authority changes.

The active Corvus system is:

- Corvus: the organization, focus, doctrine, and founder intent.
- CStar: the control plane, kernel, Hall, Augury, bead lifecycle, validation,
  and authority surface.
- Kernel: the deterministic substrate that keeps CStar from becoming only
  myth, memory, or chat.
- Researcher: the intelligence pipeline that finds signal, evidence, and
  proposals.
- Forge: the repeatable build-and-review loop composed from skills, packets,
  PMTs, workers, finalizers, receipts, and PR gates.
- Skills: bounded capabilities that can be recalled and composed, never hidden
  authority.
- Active spokes: XO and Moonshot.

## Active Spokes

### XO

XO exists because education and counsel for the founder's children are worth
real engineering. It is an active spoke when work advances child-safe learning,
guardian authority, curriculum quality, memory safety, or long-term educational
value.

XO work must treat child safety, privacy, guardian control, and age-appropriate
pedagogy as first-order requirements, not later features.

### Moonshot

Moonshot exists because the future should be built by people willing to reach
past ordinary boundaries. It is an active spoke when work advances aerospace,
autonomy, simulation, CAD truth, mission planning, verification, or a real path
toward technically credible space work.

Moonshot work must keep hard-truth gates. Beauty and ambition are not excuses
for invented numbers, weak simulations, or placeholder proof.

## Business And Parked Work

ENM is a business. It is not a gamified spoke and must not be forced into the
Forge mythology. ENM may receive business operations work through its own gates
when explicitly authorized, but it is outside the default Corvus Forge
spoke-focus lane.

All other projects are parked unless Morderith explicitly reactivates them.
Parked means silent:

- No Researcher allocation by default.
- No Forge allocation by default.
- No new PMT work by default.
- No live-fire proof work by default.
- No "while we are here" cleanup by default.
- No attempt to keep the project warm through ambient automation.

Examples of parked or watch-only surfaces include SecureSphere, CorvusEye,
Taliesin, FallowsHallowRPG, NexplayNexus, legacy game concepts, narrative
engines, broad gamified-spoke experiments, and any other spoke not named as
active in this charter.

Parked does not mean hated. Parked does not mean deleted. Parked means Corvus
will not pay attention tax until purpose returns.

## Reactivation Gate

A parked project may re-enter active scope only when all of the following are
true:

- Morderith names the project and the reason it matters now.
- The work has a clear purpose tied to Corvus, CStar, XO, Moonshot, Researcher,
  Forge, or Skills.
- CStar can represent the work as a bead, issue, acceptance criteria, and
  validation route.
- The expected cost in PMTs, workers, model spend, review time, and focus is
  explicit.
- CoS classifies the reactivation as green, yellow, or red before execution.

If the reactivation needs a new PMT, durable skill, automation, deploy,
secret/config mutation, production behavior, or main/master publication, it is
yellow or red by default.

## Researcher Law

Researcher is one of the highest-leverage organs in Corvus.

Researcher exists to bring in better truth before Corvus builds. It gathers
signals, compares the frontier, finds evidence, detects duplicates, tests
assumptions, and proposes work. It does not execute project code, merge PRs,
mutate state, or replace CStar acceptance.

Researcher must prioritize:

1. CStar and Kernel correctness.
2. Corvus Forge and skill-loop improvement.
3. Researcher pipeline quality itself.
4. XO, only when work materially improves child-safe education or counsel.
5. Moonshot, only when work materially improves hard technical truth.
6. External threats or tailwinds that directly affect the active charter.

Researcher must ignore parked projects by default. Interesting is not enough.
The question is always: does this signal matter to the active Corvus charter?

## Forge Law

Forge is not a single agent, a black box, or a license to unleash workers.
Forge is a disciplined loop.

The normal Forge route remains:

`Researcher/PPR -> CStar bead -> GitHub issue -> PMT work branch -> Hermes MiniMax SwarmForge -> worker branch -> worker PR -> PMT review -> MM summary -> CoS decision`

The Forge exists to move accepted work through reviewable artifacts. It does
not create its own authority. It does not decide what matters. It does not
consume parked projects because they look interesting. It does not merge,
publish, deploy, mutate secrets, or rewrite CStar state without explicit gates.

Forge quality is measured by:

- Clean scope.
- Exact-head discipline.
- Dirty-root isolation.
- Evidence receipts.
- Verified finalization.
- PMT review.
- CStar closeout.
- Low hallucination and overclaim risk.
- High useful work delegated to Hermes/MiniMax where safe.

The target is not to make Codex do everything. The target is to make Codex the
control and review surface while Hermes/MiniMax carries most bounded worker
load. The ratio improves only when packet quality, finalizer gates, and PMT
review stay strict.

## Skill Law

Skills are spells: bounded, named, reviewable capabilities.

A skill may help recall doctrine, assemble a packet, generate a checklist,
prepare a prompt, or standardize review. A skill must not become hidden
authority. It must not dispatch live work, install itself durably, mutate broad
PMT memory, or replace CStar bead state without separate approval.

Forge itself is best understood as a composition of skills inside an operating
loop. The loop matters more than any one skill.

## PMT Law

PMTs are project stewards. They are not the organization.

Every PMT must stay scoped to its project. PMT sprawl is a focus failure. A PMT
that cannot name its bead, issue, head, validation, worker branch, PR, and risk
classification is not ready to ask for execution or acceptance.

MM coordinates PMTs. CoS decides yellow and red routing. Morderith decides
founder-level purpose.

## Pulse Law

Pulse is an external relevance layer. It is not CoS, not Researcher, not PMT,
and not Forge.

Pulse tests whether the active Corvus charter is still relevant against the
outside world:

- Has the frontier moved?
- Is the active work becoming more valuable?
- Is a premise becoming weaker?
- Is a competitor, standard, model, tool, or platform shift changing the
  design pressure?
- Should Researcher investigate a specific threat, tailwind, or timing signal?

Pulse may challenge assumptions. Pulse may suggest investigations. Pulse may
produce tailwinds, threats, design pressure, timing signals, watch items, and
false alarms.

Pulse must not create operational backlog. It must not manage time. It must not
directly route workers. It must not cause parked projects to re-enter scope
without founder decision and CStar representation.

## Standing Scope Rule

When in doubt, the default answer is:

`CStar, Kernel, Researcher, Forge, Skills, XO, Moonshot.`

Everything else is parked, watch-only, or business-separated until Morderith
brings it back through the reactivation gate.

This is the current shape of Corvus.

Do not make the system larger until the center is strong enough to carry it.
